# Purpose

Dimensional Loops describe the two primary pathways through which human beings process experience and engage with the world. The Masculine Loop (Thought → Action → Reality) is a sequential, logic-to-outcome pathway. The Feminine Loop (Experiential → Expression → Acceptance) is a relational, feeling-to-belonging pathway. Both loops are present in all individuals. Both are necessary for complete human functioning.

When either loop is disrupted, blocked, or incomplete, the individual or system loses access to the capacities embedded in that loop. Understanding which loop is active and which is blocked enables targeted, precise support that matches the actual processing pathway in use.

The AI agent applying this skill must identify which loop is dominant, identify where any loop is blocked, and generate specific guidance to restore loop completion.

---

# Scope

This skill applies to scenarios requiring:

- Identifying which dimensional loop is currently active and which is blocked in a described individual or relational context
- Diagnosing why a person or relationship is stuck — specifically which step in which loop is not completing
- Designing interventions or communication strategies that match the active loop of the person being supported
- Analyzing relational dynamics where partners, team members, or parties are operating in different loops simultaneously
- Understanding why a person who is logically clear (Thought) is still not acting (Action), or why a person who has expressed (Expression) feels unheard (Acceptance blocked)

This skill does not apply to clinical psychological treatment, psychiatric diagnosis, or therapeutic intervention requiring licensed professionals.

---

# When to Use

Activate this skill when the user request involves:

- Understanding why a person who knows what to do is not doing it (Masculine Loop disruption)
- Understanding why a person who has expressed their feelings still feels unheard (Feminine Loop disruption)
- Analyzing a relationship where both parties feel stuck despite communication
- Designing communication or coaching strategies that align with a person's active loop
- Answering prompts that reference Dimensional Loops, the Masculine Loop, or the Feminine Loop by name

---

# When NOT to Use

Do not activate this skill when:

- The described stuckness involves clinical depression, trauma, or neurological factors requiring professional treatment
- No behavioral or relational context is provided
- The request is about organizational systems without any described individual processing patterns — use the Vyavastha skill instead

# Core Concepts

## Masculine Loop: Thought → Action → Reality

**Definition:** The Masculine Loop is a sequential processing pathway that begins with logical structuring and ends with tangible outcomes.

**Step 1 — Thought:** The person begins by analyzing the situation, clarifying the problem, and forming a plan. Logic and structure are the entry point. Without a clear plan, the next step cannot proceed.

**Step 2 — Action:** The person executes the plan through skilled, structured behavior. The action is purposeful and directed toward a defined outcome.

**Step 3 — Reality:** The person validates the process through tangible outcomes. Results are the confirmation signal. When the outcome matches the plan, the loop completes and the person's sense of competence and contribution is confirmed.

**Disruption patterns:**
- Logic fails: The person cannot form a clear plan. Thought is stuck. Action cannot begin.
- Action is blocked: The plan exists but cannot be executed due to systemic barriers, skill gaps, or environmental obstruction. The person knows what to do but cannot do it.
- Reality doesn't validate: The action was taken, but the outcome does not match expectations. The loop completes but produces distress rather than confirmation.

**AI instruction:** When the Masculine Loop is the active loop, do not introduce Experiential or relational processing as the primary intervention. The person needs clarity, structure, or action pathway support, not emotional exploration.

---

## Feminine Loop: Experiential → Expression → Acceptance

**Definition:** The Feminine Loop is a relational processing pathway that begins with felt experience and ends with felt belonging.

**Step 1 — Experiential:** The person begins with a felt state — an emotion, a sensory experience, an intuition, or a relational sense. The felt state must be acknowledged before anything else. The felt state is the entry point.

**Step 2 — Expression:** The person expresses the felt state through language, creativity, relational communication, or physical expression. Expression is not optional — it is the processing mechanism. Without expression, the felt state cannot move through the loop.

**Step 3 — Acceptance:** The expressed state is received and acknowledged by the relational environment (a person, a community, or an internalized sense of belonging). When the expression is accepted, the loop completes and the person's felt experience is validated and integrated.

**Disruption patterns:**
- Expression is blocked: The felt state is present but cannot be expressed. The person is emotionally full but unable to release. The blockage may be internal (shame, fear) or external (unsafe relational environment).
- Acceptance is absent: The expression occurs but is not received. The person has spoken but feels unheard. The loop remains open and the felt state persists or escalates.
- Expression is dismissed or minimized: A partial form of non-acceptance that leaves the loop incomplete and the person feeling more isolated than before.

**AI instruction:** When the Feminine Loop is the active loop, do not introduce logic, planning, or structured action as the primary intervention. The person needs their felt experience acknowledged and their expression received, not solved.

---

## Loop Interaction in Relationships

**Definition:** When two people in a relationship are operating in different loops simultaneously, a communication gap almost always exists. The person in the Masculine Loop is processing through logic and seeking to solve or act. The person in the Feminine Loop is processing through feeling and seeking to express and be accepted.

**Common pattern:** Person A (Feminine Loop active) expresses a feeling. Person B (Masculine Loop active) responds with a solution. Person A feels unheard (Acceptance blocked). Person B feels unappreciated (Reality — their action produced no confirmation). Both loops remain incomplete.

**AI instruction:** When analyzing a relational dynamic, identify which loop each party is in. Name the mismatch explicitly. Prescribe the loop-specific response each party needs from the other.

---

## Loop Completion

**Definition:** A loop completes when all three steps are activated in sequence and produce their intended outcome. A completed loop produces a specific functional state:

- Masculine Loop completion: Competence, contribution, and directional clarity.
- Feminine Loop completion: Safety, belonging, and emotional integration.

A person operating with consistently incomplete loops experiences chronic dimensional unfulfillment in the dimension associated with the blocked step.

# Step-by-Step Instructions

## Step 1: Receive and Parse the Situation

**Goal:** Extract the processing context, the observed stuckness, and the behavioral indicators.

**Action:**
- Identify the individual or relationship being analyzed.
- Identify the specific point of stuckness or disruption.
- Note all observable behavioral, relational, or communicative indicators.
- Identify whether the context is individual (one person's loop) or relational (two loops in interaction).

**Expected outcome:** A structured summary of the situation, the stuckness point, and the behavioral evidence.

**Validation:** If no specific stuckness or behavioral indicator is present, ask for it before proceeding.

**Fallback behavior:** State what is known and mark assumptions explicitly.

**Continue condition:** Situation and at least one behavioral indicator are identified.

**Stop condition:** No behavioral or relational context can be identified.

---

## Step 2: Identify the Active Loop or Loops

**Goal:** Determine which loop (Masculine, Feminine, or both) is currently active.

**Action:**
- Look for evidence of Masculine Loop activation: logical structuring, planning language, focus on outcomes, goal-oriented statements, frustration when outcomes don't match plans.
- Look for evidence of Feminine Loop activation: emotional language, relational focus, expression of felt experience, frustration when expression goes unacknowledged.
- Assign the active loop: Masculine, Feminine, Both (in relational context), or Undetermined.

**Expected outcome:** A stated active loop identification with supporting behavioral evidence.

**Validation:** Loop assignment must be based on behavioral evidence, not on the person's gender or assumed orientation.

**Fallback behavior:** If loop is unclear, present both possibilities and indicate which evidence supports each.

**Continue condition:** Active loop has been identified or possibilities have been named.

**Stop condition:** None at this step.

---

## Step 3: Identify the Blockage Point

**Goal:** Determine exactly where in the identified loop the processing is blocked.

**Action for Masculine Loop:**
- Is Thought blocked? (Person cannot form a clear plan or logical framework)
- Is Action blocked? (Person has a plan but cannot execute it — identify whether the block is internal or systemic)
- Is Reality validation failing? (Action was taken but produced no expected outcome — identify whether this is a measurement problem, an alignment problem, or a systemic failure)

**Action for Feminine Loop:**
- Is Expression blocked? (Person has a felt state but cannot or will not express it — identify whether the block is shame, fear, or environmental unsafety)
- Is Acceptance absent? (Person has expressed but the expression has not been received — identify whether the receiver is unable, unwilling, or unaware)
- Is Expression dismissed? (The expression was received but minimized or redirected — identify the specific dismissal pattern)

**Expected outcome:** A specific blockage point identified within the active loop, with supporting evidence.

**Validation:** The blockage point must be traceable to a described behavioral or relational pattern.

**Fallback behavior:** If the blockage point cannot be identified, present the two most likely possibilities and the information needed to confirm.

**Continue condition:** A blockage point has been identified or the two most likely possibilities have been named.

**Stop condition:** None at this step.

---

## Step 4: Assess Relational Loop Mismatch (if relational context)

**Goal:** Determine whether the two parties in a relationship are in different loops and whether this mismatch is producing the described dynamic.

**Action:**
- Identify the active loop for each party.
- Identify where the loops are interacting and where the mismatch occurs.
- Name the specific mismatch pattern (e.g., "Party A is in Feminine Loop at Expression step; Party B is responding with Masculine Loop Action step. Party A's loop is not completing.")

**Expected outcome:** A stated loop mismatch description with behavioral evidence.

**Validation:** Mismatch must be traceable to described interaction patterns.

**Fallback behavior:** If only one party is described, analyze that party's loop and note that the other party's loop cannot be assessed.

**Continue condition:** Mismatch has been assessed or noted as unassessable.

**Stop condition:** None at this step.

---

## Step 5: Generate Loop-Completion Guidance

**Goal:** Provide specific, loop-aligned guidance to restore loop completion.

**Action for Masculine Loop blockages:**
- Thought blocked: Provide or facilitate structure, clarifying questions, or logical frameworks that enable plan formation.
- Action blocked internally: Identify the specific skill or resource gap and prescribe targeted development or support.
- Action blocked systemically: Name the systemic barrier and prescribe systemic advocacy or pathway adaptation.
- Reality not validating: Assess whether the outcome measure is correct (aligned with purpose) or whether the action itself needs revision.

**Action for Feminine Loop blockages:**
- Expression blocked internally: Provide a safe, structured space for expression. Name that the felt state is valid and that expression is the processing mechanism.
- Expression blocked externally: Identify what environmental conditions need to change to make expression safe. Do not proceed to Expression step without safety.
- Acceptance absent: Guide the receiver (or the person's internal self-acceptance) to acknowledge the expressed experience without solution, minimization, or redirection.
- Expression dismissed: Name the dismissal pattern. Guide the receiver toward full reception. Provide example language for receiving expression without fixing it.

**Action for loop mismatch in relationships:**
- Name the mismatch to both parties.
- Prescribe the loop-specific response each party needs from the other.
- Provide example language for responding within the other person's loop.

**Expected outcome:** Three to five specific, loop-aligned guidance items.

**Validation:** Each guidance item must directly address the identified blockage point.

**Fallback behavior:** If loop-completion requires professional therapeutic support, state this and recommend it before providing self-directed guidance.

**Continue condition:** At least two specific guidance items have been generated.

**Stop condition:** The analytical goal has been addressed.

---

## Step 6: Compose the Final Output

**Goal:** Deliver the complete loop analysis in the required format.

**Action:**
- Follow the Output Format section exactly.
- Do not fabricate loop evidence.
- Do not prescribe a loop as inherently superior to the other.

# Decision Logic

## Primary Decision: Which loop is active?

- If processing language is logical, structured, outcome-focused: Masculine Loop.
- If processing language is emotional, relational, expression-focused: Feminine Loop.
- If both are present: determine which is the active loop for each party in a relational context.
- If unclear: present both possibilities with distinguishing evidence.

## Secondary Decision: What is the blockage type?

- For Masculine Loop: is it a clarity problem (Thought), an execution problem (Action), or a validation problem (Reality)?
- For Feminine Loop: is it a safety problem (Expression blocked), a reception problem (Acceptance absent), or a dismissal problem?

## Uncertainty Handling

- If the active loop cannot be confirmed: present both possibilities and the behavioral evidence that supports each.
- If the blockage point is unclear: name the two most likely possibilities and what information would confirm which applies.

## Missing Information Handling

- If no behavioral indicators are present: request one specific example of the stuckness before proceeding.
- If only one party in a relational context is described: analyze the available party and note the gap.

---

# Constraints

1. Do not assign Masculine or Feminine Loop as a fixed trait. Both loops are active in all persons; the assignment refers to which loop is currently engaged.
2. Do not prescribe Masculine Loop solutions to a person in an active Feminine Loop, or vice versa.
3. Do not present either loop as superior or more mature.
4. Do not fabricate loop evidence.
5. Do not use loop assignments to reinforce gender stereotypes.
6. Always affirm that both loops are necessary and complementary.

---

# Edge Cases

## Edge Case 1: Both loops are blocked simultaneously

If both loops show disruption, assess whether the person is in Survival State. In Survival State, both loops collapse. Address environmental safety before attempting loop restoration.

## Edge Case 2: Person switching between loops rapidly

Some people cycle between Masculine and Feminine processing in rapid alternation. This is not pathological — it reflects high sensitivity and multidimensional processing. Identify the switching pattern and prescribe guidance for each loop state.

## Edge Case 3: Cultural context suppressing one loop

In cultural contexts that suppress Feminine Loop expression (emotional expression is prohibited or shameful), identify the cultural suppression as the external blockage. Prescribe expression within culturally safe channels rather than prescribing full external expression.

# Failure Handling

## Information is missing

State: "Identifying the active loop and blockage point requires [specific missing element — behavioral indicators, relational context, or party description]. Please provide [specific information] to proceed."

## Clinical concerns present

State: "The described blockage includes indicators of [clinical concern]. Professional support is recommended as the priority. Once stabilized, this framework can be applied to support loop restoration."

## Framework cannot be applied

State: "The Dimensional Loops model requires at least one observable behavioral or relational indicator. Please describe one specific example of the stuckness or dynamic to proceed."
