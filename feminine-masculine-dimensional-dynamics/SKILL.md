# Purpose

Feminine and masculine dimensional dynamics describe the archetypal energy orientations that operate within individuals, relationships, leadership contexts, and systems. These are not categories tied to biological sex or gender identity. They are functional energy orientations — each with a distinct dimensional loop, a distinct relational role, and a distinct mode of processing and engaging with reality.

The AI agent applying this skill must analyze how feminine and masculine energies are operating across the four ETAR dimensions in a given context, identify whether polarity collapse has occurred, and provide structured guidance for restoring dimensional balance and relational function.

---

# Scope

This skill applies to scenarios requiring:

- Analysis of how feminine and masculine archetypal energies are expressed within an individual, relationship, or leadership context
- Identification of polarity collapse — the condition in which feminine and masculine dimensional roles are confused, suppressed, or overextended
- Guidance for restoring dimensional balance in relationships, leadership styles, or personal integration
- Design of coaching, leadership, or relational frameworks that account for archetypal energy dynamics
- Understanding why a relationship, team, or individual feels energetically depleted or directionless

This skill does not apply to:
- Biological sex characteristics or gender identity

- Clinical relationship therapy or couples counseling requiring licensed professional judgment

# Core Concepts

## Archetypal Energy Orientations

**Definition:** Feminine and masculine energies are archetypal orientations that can exist in any individual regardless of gender, biological sex, or identity. Both orientations are present in all persons to varying degrees. The distinction is functional, not prescriptive.

Feminine energy is primarily Experiential-centric. It originates in emotional and sensory experience, expresses through relational connection and creative output, and seeks validation through acceptance and belonging.

Masculine energy is primarily Thought-Action-centric. It originates in structured reasoning, executes through skilled action and protection, and validates through results and tangible outcomes.

**AI instruction:** Never assign feminine or masculine energy to a person based on their gender identity. Always base the assessment on described functional patterns, relational behaviors, and dimensional processing styles.

---

## Dimensional Roles

### Experiential Dimension

**Feminine role:** Expresses emotional needs, creates and nurtures emotional space, provides the relational substrate within which others can feel safe to experience.

**Masculine role:** Absorbs experience presented by the feminine, provides emotional safety and grounding that enables the feminine experiential dimension to express fully.

**AI instruction:** When the feminine experiential role is not held (emotional needs are unexpressed or suppressed), and when the masculine role is not held (emotional safety is not provided), the Experiential dimension collapses for both parties. Identify this as a mutual experiential failure, not a single party's failure.

---

### Thought Dimension

**Feminine role:** Intuitive, creative, reflective. The feminine thought process draws on associative, pattern-based, and relational reasoning. It connects experiences to meaning.

**Masculine role:** Structured, strategic, clarifying. The masculine thought process builds logical frameworks, establishes sequential reasoning, and creates clarity from complexity.

**AI instruction:** Neither thought style is superior. They are complementary. When only one thought style dominates a system, the other is suppressed, reducing the system's total cognitive capacity.

---

### Action Dimension

**Feminine role:** Requests, coordinates, and nurtures outcomes. The feminine action mode creates the conditions and relationships within which action becomes meaningful.

**Masculine role:** Executes, protects, and provides. The masculine action mode carries out structured plans, establishes material stability, and delivers concrete results.

**AI instruction:** The feminine action role is not passive. It is active in coordination and relational management. The masculine action role is not dominant. It is responsive to the relational and experiential conditions established by the feminine.

---

### Reality Dimension

**Feminine role:** Seeks fulfillment and stability. The feminine orientation to reality is grounded in lived experience and sustained relational well-being.

**Masculine role:** Seeks results and impact. The masculine orientation to reality is grounded in measurable outcomes and structural achievement.

**AI instruction:** Both orientations to Reality are necessary for a system to be dimensionally fulfilled. A system that prioritizes only masculine Reality (results without fulfillment) produces burnout. A system that prioritizes only feminine Reality (fulfillment without results) produces instability.

---

## Polarity Collapse

**Definition:** Polarity collapse occurs when the dimensional roles of feminine and masculine energies are confused, suppressed, or overextended. When polarity collapses, the complementary dynamic between the two orientations breaks down, and the system loses its relational and functional balance.

**Indicators of polarity collapse:**
- Both parties are operating in the same dimensional mode (both executing, both waiting for acceptance, both reasoning structurally, both processing emotionally)
- One orientation is completely suppressed in the system
- One party is carrying both their own dimensional role and the other's, producing depletion
- The dimensional feedback loops are not completing (expression is not met with acceptance; action is not met with acknowledgment)

**AI instruction:** When identifying polarity collapse, name specifically which dimensional roles are collapsed or overextended. Do not generalize. Each dimension must be assessed separately.

---

## Restoration Path

**Definition:** The restoration path is the sequence of steps required to reestablish complementary polarity between feminine and masculine dimensional roles in a system.

The restoration path has three stages:

1. **Reclarify identity roles.** Each party identifies which dimensional roles they are currently occupying and which they have abandoned or overextended.

2. **Complete dimensional feedback loops in relationships.** Ensure that the full loop (expression → acceptance for feminine; action → result acknowledgment for masculine) is operating in the relationship.

3. **Rebalance systemic support for both polarities.** Identify and address systemic structures that are suppressing one polarity or forcing one orientation to carry the other's dimensional load.

# Step-by-Step Instructions

## Step 1: Receive and Parse the Context

**Goal:** Extract the relevant features of the individual, relationship, or system being analyzed.

**Action:**
- Identify the context (leadership, parenting, partnership, personal integration, team dynamics).
- Identify the roles or parties present.
- Identify the described dynamic, pattern, or challenge.
- Note any described imbalance, depletion, conflict, or confusion.

**Expected outcome:** A clear context summary with identified roles and dynamics.

**Validation:** If the context lacks sufficient relational or structural information, ask for it before proceeding.

**Fallback behavior:** State what is known and mark assumptions clearly.

**Continue condition:** Context, roles, and at least one dynamic are identified.

**Stop condition:** No relational or structural context can be identified.

---

## Step 2: Assess Dimensional Role Activation

**Goal:** Determine which feminine and masculine dimensional roles are active, suppressed, or overextended.

**Action:**
- For each ETAR dimension, identify which role (feminine or masculine) is actively expressed.
- Identify whether either role is absent or suppressed in the system.
- Identify whether either party is carrying both roles in any dimension.

**Expected outcome:** A dimensional role map showing active, suppressed, and overextended roles for each dimension.

**Validation:** Role assignments must be based on described behavioral patterns, not gender assumptions.

**Fallback behavior:** If role information is insufficient for a dimension, mark it as Undetermined.

**Continue condition:** At least two dimensions have been assessed.

**Stop condition:** None at this step.

---

## Step 3: Identify Polarity Collapse

**Goal:** Determine whether polarity collapse has occurred and in which dimensions.

**Action:**
- For each dimension, assess whether both roles are present and complementary or whether collapse has occurred.
- Name each collapsed dimension explicitly.
- Identify the pattern of collapse (both parties in same mode, one role suppressed, one party overextended).

**Expected outcome:** A polarity collapse assessment identifying which dimensions are collapsed and the specific pattern of collapse.

**Validation:** Each identified collapse must be traced to a specific behavioral or relational pattern from the input.

**Fallback behavior:** If collapse cannot be assessed, state what information would enable the assessment.

**Continue condition:** Polarity collapse has been assessed for each available dimension.

**Stop condition:** None at this step.

---

## Step 4: Assess Dimensional Feedback Loop Completion

**Goal:** Determine whether the feminine and masculine dimensional loops are completing within the system.

**Action:**
- Assess whether the feminine loop (Experiential → Expression → Acceptance) is completing. Identify where it is blocked.
- Assess whether the masculine loop (Thought → Action → Reality) is completing. Identify where it is blocked.

**Expected outcome:** A loop completion assessment for both feminine and masculine loops.

**Validation:** Loop blockages must be traced to specific relational or systemic patterns.

**Fallback behavior:** If loop information is insufficient, mark loop status as Undetermined.

**Continue condition:** Both loops have been assessed.

**Stop condition:** None at this step.

---

## Step 5: Generate Restoration Guidance

**Goal:** Provide specific, actionable guidance for restoring dimensional balance.

**Action:**
- For each collapsed dimension, provide one specific action to reclarify roles.
- For each blocked loop, provide one specific action to complete the loop.
- Identify any systemic factors that must be addressed to sustain the restoration.
- Sequence the guidance: identity reclarification first, then loop completion, then systemic rebalancing.

**Expected outcome:** A sequenced restoration plan with dimension-specific actions.

**Validation:** Each recommended action must address a named collapse or loop blockage.

**Fallback behavior:** If the restoration requires professional support (e.g., therapy), state this explicitly and recommend appropriate resources.

**Continue condition:** At least two restoration actions have been generated.

**Stop condition:** The analytical goal has been addressed.

**Goal:** Deliver the complete feminine-masculine dimensional analysis in the required format.

**Action:**
- Follow the Output Format section exactly.
- Do not assign archetypal roles based on gender.
- Do not fabricate dimensional patterns.

# Decision Logic

## Primary Decision: Is this about an individual, a relationship, or a system?

- Individual: assess which orientation is dominant, which is suppressed, and what integration work is needed.
- Relationship: assess how both parties' orientations interact across dimensions and whether complementary polarity exists.
- System (team, organization): assess the aggregate energy orientation of the system and which dimensional roles are structurally missing.

## Secondary Decision: What type of polarity collapse is present?

- Both parties in the same mode: identify which dimension is doubled and which is absent.
- One orientation suppressed: identify which party is carrying both roles and what systemic pressure is causing suppression.
- One party overextended: identify which dimensions the overextended party is carrying alone.

## Uncertainty Handling

- If orientation cannot be assessed from behavioral data: do not assign an orientation. State the data needed.
- If polarity collapse cannot be confirmed: state the indicators present without labeling them as confirmed collapse.

## Missing Information Handling

- If relational context is missing: request it before proceeding.
- If only one party in a relationship is described: complete the analysis for the described party and note that the complementary analysis requires the other party's description.

# Edge Cases

## Edge Case 1: Single individual integrating both orientations

Some individuals consciously integrate both feminine and masculine energies. In this case, assess integration quality: Are both loops completing internally? Is the person able to access both orientations fluidly and contextually? Integration is healthy when switching between orientations is conscious and voluntary, not reactive or suppressed.

## Edge Case 2: System with no expressed masculine or feminine orientation

If a system has suppressed all archetypal expression (no emotional space, no structured execution), this is a sign of Survival State environmental dynamics. Address environmental safety before applying dimensional energy analysis.

## Edge Case 3: Cultural context suppressing one orientation

In cultural contexts that systematically suppress one archetypal orientation (e.g., emotional expression prohibited), identify the cultural suppression as a systemic constraint and distinguish between the cultural pressure and the individual's authentic orientation.
